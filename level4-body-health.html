⚠ jèp vs bpùat

เจ็บ (jèp) is a sharp, immediate pain — it comes after the body part: "kǎa jèp" (my leg hurts). ปวด (bpùat) is a duller, ongoing ache — it comes before the body part instead: "bpùat-hǔa" (headache), "bpùat-tháwng" (stomachache). Same idea, opposite word order — worth getting right since a pharmacist will react differently to each.

How to drill this

  1. Touch each body part as you say it — the physical anchor makes these stick faster than any other section in the suite.
  2. Practice "___ jèp" and "bpùat-___" with three different body parts each, out loud, until the word order is automatic.
  3. Say all six emergency phrases from memory, with no table in front of you, until you can do it without thinking.

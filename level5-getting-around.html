⚠ metered vs negotiated

A metered taxi (rót-táek-sîi) should run the meter — ask with "bpèrt-mí-dtêr-dûai" and if they refuse, it's fine to just get another cab. Motorbike taxis (win) don't use meters at all — the orange-vest drivers at a soi-mouth stand have fixed short-hop rates, but always confirm the price before you get on, not after.

How to drill this

  1. Next time you take any ride, say "bpai tîi ___" out loud with the real destination before you show it on your phone.
  2. Practice asking "rót àwk gìi mohng" and "ใช้เวลากี่ชั่วโมง" together — departure time and duration are almost always asked as a pair.
  3. Rehearse the metered-vs-negotiated callout as a mini script: ask for the meter, and if refused, practice agreeing a number before stepping in.

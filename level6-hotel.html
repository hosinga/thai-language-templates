⚠ the missing 4th floor

สี่ (sìi, "four") sounds close enough to death-associated words that many Thai and Thai-Chinese buildings skip a labeled 4th floor entirely, the same superstition that skips 13 in Western buildings. If your room confirmation jumps from floor 3 to floor 5, that's why — not a booking error.

How to drill this

  1. Rehearse the whole check-in exchange as one sequence: booking name, key, room number, checkout time.
  2. Pick one room-problem template and actually use it next time something's genuinely off — real use beats repetition.
  3. Learn "mii ___ mǎi" (do you have ___?) from Level 0's shopping section as your universal amenity question — wifi, breakfast, extra towels, all the same pattern.

⚠ no conjugation, anywhere

Thai verbs have exactly one form — gin is "eat," "eats," "ate," and "eating," always. Time and completion are carried by separate marker words placed before or after the verb: จะ (jà) before it signals the future, แล้ว (láew) after it signals it's already done. Drop the verb into a template unchanged and just add the right marker.

How to drill this

  1. Take one verb and say it three ways: plain, with jà in front, and with láew after — hear how only the marker changes.
  2. Chain "yàak bpai" (want to go) onto any place name you already know from earlier levels.
  3. Narrate your actual day for thirty seconds using nothing but verbs from this table plus markers.

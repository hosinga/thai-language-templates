⚠ big notes, small vendors

A 1,000-baht note (the largest common bill) is a genuine problem at a street stall, a tuk-tuk, or a small shop first thing in the morning before they've made change. Break large notes at a 7-Eleven or a bank before you need small bills, and keep "báeng-yôi mii mǎi" ready for when you're caught out anyway.

How to drill this

  1. Run a full negotiation out loud, start to finish, using Level 0's opener plus three templates from this page.
  2. Practice reading a price back to confirm it — say the number using Level 0's numbers table before you hand over any cash.
  3. Keep a running mental note of your smallest bill and coin count — it's the single most useful habit for daily transactions.

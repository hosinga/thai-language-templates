⚠ the kráp / kâ split

Thai has no single word for "please" that works for everyone — politeness rides on a sentence-ending particle, and that particle is gendered. Men end statements and questions with kráp. Women end statements with kâ and questions with ká (rising tone, easy to miss). Get the wrong one and it just sounds off — not rude, just noticeably not how a Thai speaker of your gender would say it.

How to drill this

  1. Get sà-wàt-dii plus your own gender particle automatic first — that's most of every first interaction.
  2. Say the introduction templates as one full sentence with your real name, hometown, and age slotted in, out loud, not just the blank.
  3. Keep "mâi kâo-jai" and "pôot cháa-cháa dâi mǎi" ready — together they buy you time in any conversation.
